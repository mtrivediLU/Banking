First 90 days

How I would create value without rushing change

My first priority would be to understand the team's current state before proposing changes. In a bank environment, credibility comes from listening, respecting controls, and improving reliability with discipline.

90 day value plan

Days 1 to 30

Listen, map, and build context

Understand CBO Performance Enablement priorities, key stakeholders, current data assets, support pain points, controls, and team operating rhythm.

Days 31 to 60

Stabilize and strengthen

Identify quick reliability wins: pipeline monitoring, reconciliation checks, documentation gaps, backlog hygiene, and production support playbooks.

Days 61 to 90

Deliver visible improvement

Ship one or two tangible improvements that improve reporting trust, reduce manual work, or shorten issue resolution time for downstream teams.

Reliability checklist

  • Freshness checks on critical datasets.
  • Row count and financial total reconciliation.
  • Clear incident triage and escalation paths.
  • Source to report lineage documentation.
  • Code review and deployment discipline.

Manager behaviours

  • Listen before redesigning.
  • Clarify team priorities and decision rights.
  • Make work visible through clear backlog hygiene.
  • Protect the team from constant context switching.
  • Coach through examples, not only instructions.

What I would not do immediately

I would not arrive with a prebuilt architecture and force it onto the team. I would first learn the bank's standards, current platform constraints, governance expectations, and stakeholder pain points. Then I would propose improvements that fit Scotiabank's risk culture and operating model.
